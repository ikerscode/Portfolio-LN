02 / Case Study

VANTAGE

A self-hosted satellite-imagery workbench for defence and ISR analysts.

Analysts burn hours pulling imagery by hand, flipping between dates, and eyeballing what moved. VANTAGE does that part for you. Pick an area, and it fetches the imagery, lines the dates up, and tells you what changed.

It runs against Sentinel-2 (10 m optical, 13 bands, roughly five-day revisit) and Sentinel-1 (C-band radar, so it sees through cloud and at night). Put a monitor on an area and it re-checks on a schedule, then flags you when something crosses a threshold.

A few calls I made
  • Radar as well as optical, because half the areas worth watching are under cloud exactly when you need them.
  • Detection tuned at Sentinel-2's 10 m resolution, not borrowed from a model trained on sharp photos that falls apart on satellite pixels.
  • Self-hosted by default, so the imagery and the analysis stay on the operator's own infrastructure.
Where I drew the line

VANTAGE reports what's there and what changed, and nothing past that. No targeting, no fire-control, no strike planning, no sensor-to-shooter automation. It's a tool for understanding a place. If you want software to help decide where to look, that's the job. If you want it to help decide where to shoot, that's a different tool, and I didn't build it.
